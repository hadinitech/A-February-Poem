The Story

A letter written
in winter light

Some books find you when you least expect them. A February Poem is not written to be read, it is written to be felt. Line by quiet line, Glenda Muthuhadini unfolds a memoir of love that ended before she understood what she had, of grief that arrived with no invitation, and of healing that happened slowly, the way February thaws into March.

This is a book about the ordinary moments that shatter you, morning coffees grown cold, unanswered messages, the particular silence of a shared bed made for one. It is about learning to hold your own story with tenderness, without shame.

"for everyone who lost something in winter..."

Written across two years of personal reckoning, these poems breathe with the rawness of a journal and the grace of a literary gift, something rare, something necessary, something you will press into the hands of people you love.

The Author

Written from the
inside of things

Glenda Muthuhadini writes from the quiet spaces, the pauses between words, the silences after doors close. A South African writer who has always believed that poetry is not an art form, but a survival form, she began writing A February Poem during the coldest season of her life.

What began as private entries, words pressed into notebooks at 2am, lines that never expected to be read, slowly became something she could not hold alone. Glenda shaped those fragments into a collection that is part confession, part love letter, part gentle instruction on how to carry loss without letting it carry you.

She lives in Johannesburg, where she writes, teaches creative writing workshops to young women, and believes that every ordinary life holds an extraordinary story. This is hers.
